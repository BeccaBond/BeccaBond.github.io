Electric Go Kart

Summary

My classmate and I discovered this go kart's chassis at a flea market and decided to turn it into our personal reconstruction project. We wired and installed a new motor, motor controller, boost converter, batteries, headlights and a power switch. We manufactured new brake keys and axle spacers, modified the chassis to hold the new electronics, and optimized the gear ratio between the motor and rear axle.

Over the course of a semester, we more than doubled the kart's top speed! This took a lot of trial and error: we iterated through multiple combinations of voltage boost and gear ratio, blew and replaced electronics, disassembled and reassembled the rear axle, and took endless test laps around campus. The kart's lap time at the start of the semester was over a minute; by the end of the semester, we were taking laps in only 25 seconds.

Through working on the go kart, I practiced resilience and adaptability ("try, try again") while integrating electronic and mechanical systems. Check out our project paper below! ↓

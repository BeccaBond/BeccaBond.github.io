Sustainable Fishkeeping

Summary

Two of my classmates and I created this non-technical project to learn and practice more sustainable methods in keeping a fish. We adopted a veiltail betta whom we named "Sonic" (I later named a terrestrial rover platform after Sonic) and researched important subjects in fishkeeping such as:

  • The three stages of mechanical filtration and how to make them more efficient
  • Artificial filtration using aquatic plants and volcanic substrate
  • The nitrogen cycle and minimizing toxic chemical compounds

While I initially created this project very whimsically, aiming simply to make a new aquatic friend, I surprised myself with the amount of fishkeeping knowledge I learned over the semester. Check out our learnings in our paper below! ↓

Postscript: At the end of the semester, I traveled home across the country - and brought Sonic with me. He toured Boston, Dallas and Phoenix; made friends with the TSA agents; and became the highest fish in the world for a few hours. My next goal is to send a fish to space.
